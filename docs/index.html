What we tested

For every booking, we took the same set of partners notified in reality (the notification pool) and asked: if DA OS had chosen the offer order, which partner would have won — and what would installs look like? No change to bookings, notification pools, or partner behaviour. Only the routing priority changes.

How DA OS picks the partner

For every booking, each partner in the notification pool is ranked by a three-key priority. The booking is offered in that order; the first partner to accept wins.

PriorityCriterionRationale
1. Compliance tier Compliant / At-Risk before Non-Compliant Protect customer outcomes; starve non-compliant supply
2. Partner category HH → HL → LH → LL (SLA × Engagement) Move volume to higher-quality supply segments
3. Category-conditional quality signal HH & HL (already >80% SLA): install-rate first, SLA second
LH & LL (variable SLA): SLA first, install-rate second		 Sharpest available lever per segment — install-rate for the SLA-safe top, SLA for the SLA-risky bottom

Cascade and caps

  • Each booking cascades up to 3 partners in rank order; first to accept wins.
  • Acceptance uses each partner's own historical notif → intent ratio, Bayesian-smoothed (Beta(8,2) prior) with a 70% floor to prevent false rejection of sparse-history partners.
  • Install rate uses each partner's own historical booking → install rate.
  • Soft volume cap per partner = max(1.5 × current weekly wins, floor) with floor 10 / 5 / 3 / 2 by active-base size — prevents over-concentration.

Reading the table

  1. Volume migrates decisively to HH. HH booking share rises 36% → 51% (+15 pp), funded primarily by LL (-13 pp) and LH (-5 pp). DA OS is doing its primary job — concentrating supply on partners who keep customers.
  2. Install lift is both compositional and within-category. HH conversion improves +7.4 pp within category (the install-rate-first tiebreaker picks the stronger-converting HH partner in each pool), and HH owns 15 pp more of the booking base. Together, HH's install share jumps from 40% → 61% — where the +68 installs come from.
  3. Within-category conversion holds or improves across HH, LH, LL. HH +7.4 pp, LH +1.5 pp, LL +4.2 pp. HL dips −2.8 pp — a pool-mix effect: HL wins shift toward HL-exclusive pools where HL beats LH/LL by category priority, even when the LH/LL partner would have converted slightly better on that specific lead. Net ~11-install haircut, easily absorbed by the HH lift.
  4. Quality of installing partner improves +2.7 pp. The SLA of the partner who actually installs rises 82.2% → 84.9%. A real customer-experience dividend on top of the install count, and the clearest justification for the category-conditional SLA tiebreaker in LH/LL.
  5. No change in booking volume or supply base. Same 2,306 bookings, same notification pools, same partner universe. The lift is pure prioritisation.

Recommendation

Productionise DA OS with the ranking above

Expected outcome: +5.8% installs and +2.7 pp SLA of installing partner, with no change to supply coverage or demand volume.

Monitor in the first 4 weeks post-launch

HL / LL disengagement
De-prioritised partners may reduce app activity. Watch weekly active partner count in those segments.
HH cap utilisation
If HH partners hit caps frequently, the lift compresses. Caps may need widening in HH-dense zones.
Escalation rate
Baseline 6.3% in simulation (no top-3 acceptance → fallback). Alarm if >10% sustained.
